  • Our Humble Beginnings

    Iligan City Jail is located at Brgy. Tipanoy, Iligan City. This correctional facility has been managed by the Bureau of Jail Management and Penology under the flagship of the Department of Interior and Local Government which is a national government office.

  • An Agency is Born

    The said jail has a total population of 308 inmates as of the month of October 2010 and it has a workforce of 36 jail guards. It’s main facility is a two story building consisting 16 cells for male inmates while the 22 female inmates are housed separately in just one cell. Also inside the perimeter, it has a basketball court, a meeting hall, visitor’s reception hall, dirty kitchen, and a small backyard garden.

  • Transition to Full Service

    Iligan City Jail was recognized and awarded as the “Best City Jail” in Northern Mindanao last 2010. The facilities and amenities of the jail greatly improved. Sanitation, ventilation and water supply problems were given remedies. Playground has now concreted; walls are now elevated, barbed and fortified. the city government also deployed three nurses to attend the health needs of the inmates. Also, the new warden already submitted a budget proposal to the City Budget Office.

  • Phase Two Expansion

    Its morale is currently high seeing a brighter hope in running the jail with a strong partnership of Iligan City Government and its constituents.

MISSION

To enhance public safety by ensuring humane safekeeping and development of Persons Deprived of Liberty (PDL) in all district, city, and municipal jails for their integration to society.

VISION

A premier institution highly regarded by society for the secure and humane treatment of Persons Deprived of Liberty (PDL) by its competent and motivated corps.

INSTRUCTIONS AND PERMISSIBLE INSIDE THE JAIL

Please read the details below before visiting the jail.

Security

  • • A valid ID is checked upon entering.
  • • They check the food you bring.
  • • Do a body search
Further instructions and information
  • • Start visition time every Tuesday and Friday is 12PM
  • • No visitation is allowed every Monday but the visitor can bring food for his related prisoner.
  • • Saturday and Sunday is Family Day and visitation starts at 8am.
  • • Children below 13 years old are only allowed to get inside during Family Day of the inmate together with his/her legal guardian.
  • • During Family Day, only those who are a direct family member of the inmate are only allowed to get inside the jail (maximum of 3 persons).

PROHIBITED INSIDE THE JAIL

Please read the details below before visiting the jail.

Clothing

  • • Yellow, orange, and camouflage clothes are not allowed to be worn by visitors.
  • • Sleeveless, tops, shorts, skirts and other revealing clothes.
  • • Jackets, caps, bonnets and shades.

Electronic devices are not allowed inside the jail.


Food

  • • Canned goods, bulad, ginamos and uyap.

Belongings

  • • Stainless things like spoon, fork and knife.
  • • Fragile and other things that are easily to break.
  • • Bottled colognes and perfumes (spray and pressurized container)
  • • Knife, scissors, ice pick, swiss knife and other sharp things.

Firearms and explosive devices.


Drugs and Beverages

  • • Shabu, cocaine, marijuana and other forbidden drugs.
  • • Cigarettes and alcoholic beverages.
More instructions:
  • • A person who is drunk or had taken alcoholic beverages upon the day of visiting is not allowed to get inside the jail.
